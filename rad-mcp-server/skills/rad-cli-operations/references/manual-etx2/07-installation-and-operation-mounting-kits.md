## RM-35  *(p.303)*

Accessories 
RM-35 
Mounting Kits for Installing 8.5-inch Devices in 19-inch and 23-inch Racks 
Product page > 
General Availability 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
This document describes the installation of 8.5” units in 19”and 23” racks using the following mounting kits: 
• RM-35/A for installing a single device (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack, regular installation (see Figure 1) 
• RM-35/A for installing a single deeper device (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack, deep installation  
(see Figure 2) 
• RM-35/P1 for installing a single device (data interfaces facing the back side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 3)  
• RM-35/P1 for installing a single deeper device (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 4)  
• RM-35-23/P1 for installing a single device (data interfaces facing the back side of the rack) in a 23” rack (see Figure 5)  
• RM-35/A2 for installing two devices (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 6) 
• RM-35/P2 for installing two devices (data interfaces facing the back side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 7)  
• RM-35/P2 for installing two deeper devices (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 8)  
• RM-35-23 for installing two devices (data interfaces facing the back side of the rack) in a 23” rack (see Figure 9)  
 
Note: Different products have different enclosure sizes and their data ports face the front or back of rack cabinets. While this document shows all types of 
RM-35 kits, it might not cover all combinations of existing product enclosures. Therefore, when selecting a mounting kit, always consult the product data 
sheet in addition to this document. 
 
This document uses the following terms: 
• Deeper device – a device with depth larger than 152.5 mm/6.0 inch 
• Deep installation – installing a device with its front panel deeper than the front rack edge. 
All the figures are drawn with the rack facing you.

Installing a Single 8.5-inch Unit in a 19” rack 
The following kits are available for this procedure: 
• RM-35/A for installing a single device (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack, regular installation (see Figure 1) 
• RM-35/A for installing a single deeper device (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack, deep installation  
(see Figure 2) 
• RM-35/P1 for installing a single device (data interfaces facing the back side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 3)  
• RM-35/P1 for installing a single deeper device (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 4)  
 
To install a single unit (RM-35/A, front installation): 
1. 
Place the unit with its connectors and LEDs facing you. 
2. 
Fasten the short bracket to the left side of the unit using two 6-32 UNC screws (see Figure 1). 
3. 
Fasten the long bracket to the right side of the unit. 
4. 
Place the unit in the rack and fasten the brackets to the side rails of the rack using M5 or M6 Phillips screws on each side (not 
included in the kit).

Installing Two Units in a 19-inch Rack 
The following kits are available: 
• RM-35/A2 for installing two devices side-by-side (data interfaces facing the front side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 6) 
• RM-35/P2 for installing two devices side-by-side (data interfaces facing the back side of the rack) in a 19” rack (see Figure 7)

1. 
Place the units with their connectors and LEDs facing you. 
2.

Figure 1). 
3. 
Attach the adapters to the inner panels using eight 6-32 UNC screws (see Figure 6).  
4. 
Fasten the two units together by sliding the side adapters into each other. Make sure that the protrusions of the adapters enter 
their corresponding slots. 
5. 
Secure the two units together by using four 4-40 UNC screws inserted into the top and bottom of each side adapter.

INSTALLATION PROCEDURE 
 
Warning 
Disconnect all cables including the power cord from the product unit before performing the 
mounting procedures. 
 
 
To install the unit on a wall: 
1. 
Mark the drilling points on the wall, using the wall-mounting bracket as a template. The bracket must be fastened to the wall 
with at least four screws. 
2. 
Fasten the bracket to the wall using the appropriate screws. 
3. 
Attach the four Philips-head bolts and spacers to the unit via the appropriate holes on the side panels, depending on the 
required installation type: cables facing up or cables facing down. See Figure 1 and Figure 2 for the detailed views. 
4. 
Insert the unit into the bracket by sliding the spacers through the open slots on the side panels of the bracket. The four spacer-
bolt assemblies (two on each side) provide support for the enclosure. 
5. 
Fasten the unit to the bracket using the flat-head bolts on the side panels of the bracket. 
6. 
Pass the power and interface cables through the conduit on the bracket.
